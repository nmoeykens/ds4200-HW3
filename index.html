Quantum Mechanics

Quantum Mechanics is the study of particles that do not follow classical physics and is governed probabilistically rather than deterministically. The subject is mainly configured using extensive linear algebra and contains a lot of the same theories and applications that come from more general fields. Quantum computing is also a large part of the study of quantum mechanics. This can be somewhat similar to classical computer science as there are logic gates that decide algorithms and languages, but in the case of quantum mechanics, bits are represented by 2x2 matrices called qubits. A quantum system is often described by vector spaces which contain the relevant information about a particle. Interestingly, the position of a state can be represented by a 3d sphere, with some combination of polar coordinates. Perhaps the most unique thing about quantum mechanics is that measuring a system will fundamentally change that system in some unpredictable way. This sounds like a problem, but is actually the main reason why people and companies are trying to build a quantum computer. The fact that measurements changes the state of a particle means that with entanglement, many things that are entirely impossible in classical computers and physics now become possible.
